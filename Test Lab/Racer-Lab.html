Confounding Variable Information

In order to evaluate whether the car speeds are truly different, we should take into account possible confounding variables (variables that the researcher did not include in the study but that might be connected to both the independent variable and the dependent variable). For example, some race tracks are longer than others. We want to hold the track constant for everyone in your experiment to ensure that the type of track does not bias our results. Are there any other potential confounding variables that might get in the way of determining which car is faster?
